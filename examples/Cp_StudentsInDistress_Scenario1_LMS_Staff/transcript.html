Students in Distress: Parent Initiated Call Transcript

Please complete the following scenario. Remember that the objectives of this training are to identify the signs and symptoms, determine the type of abuse, and review the proper reporting protocol.

The scenario provides an all too common situation that you should be prepared to report based on real-life situations. Take the knowledge gained from this scenario and remember that this could also occur via e-mail, WebMail, or even through the Message Boards.

An image of a desk appears. There are two binders on the desk, an Identifying Suspected Abuse binder and a Company Policies binder. There is also a telephone and laptop on the desk. The home screen on the laptop shows the following icons from left to right:

  • LiveLesson
  • IM
  • Connexus
  • Calendar
  • Email

The telephone is selected, which appears full screen.

(phone ringing)

<

EMPLOYEE: Hello, thank you for calling Connections Education. This is Matt. How may I help you today?

FATHER: Hi, this is Brian Demo. I'm calling since I've received three notices about returning my son's equipment.

EMPLOYEE: OK, Mr. Demo. Let me take a look into this for you. Can you please tell me your son's name?

FATHER: His name is Jaymes Demo.

EMPLOYEE: Great,let me take a look at your account.

(The father shouts to his son Jaymes in the background.)

FATHER: Jaymes! Get your butt down here, NOW!

(Jaymes responds to his father in the background.)

JAYMES: No, I don’t want to! You come up here!

(The father puts down the phone and runs up the stairs after Jaymes. There are shuffling noises and an altercation occurs. The father is choking his son.)

OTHER SIBLING: Dad! Stop it! He can’t breathe. You’re choking him! Please stop!

TEACHER: Mr. Demo? Mr. Demo? Are you there? Hello?

(The father comes back down the stairs and picks the phone back up.)

FATHER: Hello, I’m… I’m back. 

EMPLOYEE: Mr. Demo, I think I found your account. Can you please verify your address for me?

FATHER: Ummm... 674 Honeymill Lane, Grand Rapids, Michigan. I have to go. This is not a good time.

phone hangs up

The call ends, and the desk space reappears.

A hint box appears with the text: Refer to the Identifying Suspected Abuse binder to research your initial concerns about this call. Is this abuse?

The Identifying Suspected Abuse binder is selected, and the binder cover appears.

The binder is selected again, and it opens to the first page.

On-screen text appears: Introduction to Identifying the Signs and Symptoms of Child Abuse—As a mandated reporter, you need to understand the different types of child abuse and the signs and symptoms associated with each. Instructions— Select each tab to learn more about each type of abuse. When you are done researching, select the Done Researching button.

Along the edge of the binder are seven tabs:

  • cyberbullying
  • depression
  • emotional abuse
  • neglect
  • physical abuse
  • sexual abuse
  • suicide

The cyberbullying tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Cyberbullying

The Cyberbullying Research Center defines cyberbullying as “deliberate and repeated harm inflicted through the use of computers, cell phones, and other electronic devices” (from Bullying Beyond the Schoolyard: Preventing and Responding to Cyberbullying).

The link to open the Cyberbullying Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The depression tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Depression

Mental Health American describes depression as “a serious health problem that affects feelings, thoughts, and actions, and can appear as a physical illness. Depression is a mental disorder.”

The link to open the Depression Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The emotional abuse tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Emotional Abuse/Mental Injury

The Child Welfare Information Gateway defines emotional abuse as “a pattern of behavior that impairs a child’s emotional development or sense of self-worth.”

The link to open the Emotional Abuse/Mental Injury Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The neglect tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Neglect

According to the Child Welfare Information Gateway, neglect is the failure of a parent, guardian, or other caregiver to provide for a child’s basic needs.

The link to open the Neglect Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The physical abuse tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Physical Abuse

The Child Welfare Information Gateway describes physical abuse as non-accidental physical injury, which can range from bruises to severe fractures or death. Physical abuse can be inflicted by a parent, caregiver, other person, or even self-inflicted.

The link to open the Physical Abuse Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The sexual abuse tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Sexual Abuse

The Federal Child Abuse Prevention and Treatment Act defines sexual abuse as “the employment, use, persuasion, inducement, enticement, or coercion of any child to engage in, or assist any other person to engage in, any sexually explicit conduct or simulation.”

The link to open the Sexual Abuse Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The suicide tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Suicide

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), suicide is “the death caused by self-directed injurious behavior with any intent to die as a result of this behavior.”

The link to open the Suicide Signs and Symptoms job aid is selected. The job aid opens in another window.

The Done Researching button is selected, and a review question appears.

The question text reads: Which type(s) of abuse might this student be suffering from? Select all that apply.

There are four answer choices:

  • neglect
  • physical abuse
  • emotional abuse/mental injury
  • sexual abuse

The physical abuse option is selected, and then the Check Answer button is selected.

Correct feedback appears: The student suffered physical abuse since you heard the other child in the home state that Jaymes was choked.

The user is prompted to select the Continue button.

The desk space reappears.

A hint box appears with the text: Refer to the Reporting Protocols binder for protocol on how to handle this situation.

The Reporting Protocols binder is selected, and the binder cover appears.

The binder is selected again, and it opens to the first page.

On-screen text appears: Understanding Imminent and Non-Imminent Danger—Once you understand the type of abuse or neglect a child may be suffering from, you then need to determine the seriousness of the situation in order to follow the proper protocol. Instructions—Select each tab to learn more about imminent and non-imminent danger. When you are done researching, select the Done Researching button.

Along the edge of the binder are two tabs:

  • Imminent Danger
  • Non-Imminent Danger

The Imminent Danger tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Imminent Danger

Imminent danger refers to first-hand, real-time reported information versus second-hand/hearsay, and is an immediate threat of harm.

It is considered imminent danger when a student’s apparent perpetrator intends to cause harm, or such danger exists which could reasonably be expected to cause death or serious physical harm.

The Non-Imminent Danger tab is selected.

On-screen text appears:

Non-Imminent Danger

Non-imminent danger situations are concerning, but the student is not in immediate danger or in a life-threatening situation that would require a call to emergency services.

The Done Researching button is selected, and a review question appears.

The question text reads: What type of danger is this student in?

There are two answer choices:

  • imminent danger
  • non-imminent danger

The imminent danger option is selected, and then the Check Answer button is selected.

Correct feedback appears: The student’s immediate safety is in jeopardy and it is your responsibility to execute the correct protocol.  

The user is prompted to select the Continue button.

The desk space reappears.

Onscreen text appears

The proper reporting protocols for both imminent and non-imminent danger shoudl always be followed.

Select the link to review the correct protocol for imminent danger

The user is prompted to select the link and open the proper reporting protocol.

The user is prompted to select the Continue button.
